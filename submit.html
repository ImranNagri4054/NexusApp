Manuscript Preparation

Manuscript Structure

  • Title Page: Include title, author names, affiliations, corresponding author email, and ORCID IDs
  • Abstract: Maximum 300 words summarizing objectives, methods, results, and conclusions
  • Keywords: 4–6 keywords for indexing purposes
  • Main Text: Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusion
  • References: Follow APA, MLA, or Chicago Style consistently
  • Supplementary Materials: Optional appendices, datasets, or additional figures

Formatting Requirements

Document Format
  • File format: .doc, .docx, or .pdf
  • Font: Times New Roman, 12pt
  • Line spacing: Double-spaced
  • Margins: 1 inch (2.54 cm) on all sides
  • Page numbers: Bottom right corner
Figures & Tables
  • High resolution (minimum 300 dpi)
  • Formats: JPEG, PNG, TIFF, or EPS
  • Each figure/table on separate page
  • Clear captions and labels
  • Referenced in main text

Article Types

Research Articles

Full-length original research papers presenting substantial new findings.

  • Length: 5,000–8,000 words
  • Abstract: 250–300 words

Review Articles

Comprehensive analyses of recent developments and trends in a specific research area.

  • Length: 5,000–8,000 words
  • Abstract: 250–300 words

Short Communications

Brief reports of significant preliminary or novel research findings.

  • Length: 5,000–8,000 words
  • Abstract: 250–300 words

Case Studies

In-depth examinations of specific cases offering practical or theoretical insights.

  • Length: 5,000–8,000 words
  • Abstract: 250–300 words

Submission Process

Online Submission System

All submissions must be made through our online submission system. You will need to create an account if you haven't already.

Access Submission Portal

Review Timeline

  • Initial screening: 3–5 business days
  • Peer review: 4–6 weeks
  • Revision period: 2–4 weeks (if required)
  • Final decision: 1–2 weeks after revision
  • Publication: 2–4 weeks after acceptance

Communication

All correspondence regarding your submission will be sent via email. You can also track your manuscript status through your author dashboard.
